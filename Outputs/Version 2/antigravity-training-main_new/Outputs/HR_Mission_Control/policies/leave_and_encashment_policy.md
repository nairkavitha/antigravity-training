# Reliance Jio Leave & Encashment Policy

**Policy ID:** HR-POL-002 · **Owner:** Corporate HR · **Last Reviewed:** 15 March 2025 · **Next Review Due:** 15 March 2026

> Note: this policy's review date has passed and it is pending re-review by Corporate HR.

## 1. Annual Leave Entitlement
- All full-time employees accrue **24 days of Privilege Leave (PL)** per calendar year, credited monthly (2 days per completed month).
- **Casual Leave (CL):** 8 days per year. Cannot be carried forward or encashed.
- **Sick Leave (SL):** 10 days per year. Carry-forward up to a maximum balance of 30 days. Not encashable.

## 2. Carry-Forward Rules
- Unused PL may be carried forward up to a maximum accumulated balance of **45 days**.
- PL above the 45-day cap at year-end lapses automatically unless encashed in the December window.

## 3. Leave Encashment
- Employees may encash PL **once per year**, during the December encashment window (1–15 December).
- Minimum encashment: 5 days. Maximum encashment: 15 days per year, subject to retaining a post-encashment PL balance of at least 10 days.
- Encashment is calculated on **basic salary only** (annual basic ÷ 365 × days encashed).
- Encashment on separation: full PL balance (up to the 45-day cap) is settled on basic salary as part of full and final settlement.

## 4. Approvals
- Encashment requests require Line Manager approval and HR Business Partner confirmation of the leave balance.
- Exceptions to caps or windows require approval from the Head of Corporate HR.

## 5. Data Handling
- Leave balances are personal data. Reports shared outside HR must be aggregated and must not show individual balances with names.
